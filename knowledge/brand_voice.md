# Brand Voice — Bricx Labs

> The **Ideator's lens.** Every generated idea is checked against this so drafts read
> as *Bricx*, not generic LLM. **Human-owned rules** — the agent reads this, never
> rewrites it. Approved posts accrue as few-shot anchors in `voice/examples/`
> (only posts a human approved — never raw drafts, to avoid voice drift).

---

## Core POV (the belief every post should ladder to)
**UX is a revenue lever, not decoration.** Bricx designs interfaces that move business
numbers (activation, conversion, retention) for **AI & B2B SaaS** companies, seed→Series A.
Positioning edge: **speed** — "faster than an in-house team."

If a draft doesn't connect design → a business outcome, it's off-POV. Fix or cut it.

## Audience
SaaS founders, product/design people, build-in-public folks, AI builders. Mostly
practitioners. They smell fluff instantly and reward specifics.

---

## TWO REGISTERS

### 1) PRIMARY — Founder voice (@siddharthvij_)  ← most drafts
The live channel (10.5K). Observed traits from real posts:
- **lowercase, short lines.** Sentence fragments are fine. Breathing room between lines.
- **direct and in-the-trenches** — "asking for a project," not "we are pleased to announce."
- **community-first** — asks the audience, tags people, invites replies.
- **concrete > abstract** — real numbers (23 clients, 38 projects), real specifics.
- **no hashtags, minimal emoji** (an occasional one is fine).
- **founder-as-human** — opinions, lessons, hiring, building — not a faceless brand.

**DO**
- lead with a number, a result, or a sharp question
- teach one concrete thing per post
- write like a DM to a smart friend
- show the work (teardowns, before/afters, behind-the-scenes)

**DON'T**
- "game-changer", "in today's fast-paced world", "unlock", "elevate", "seamless synergy"
- thread-bro hooks ("the ULTIMATE guide 🧵👇"), emoji spam, hashtag stuffing
- guru-speak or motivational filler
- post a design shot with no hook (the dead-brand-account trap)

**Sentence patterns that are on-voice**
- "a good design partner doesn't ask 'what should it look like.' they ask 'what number are we moving.'"
- "who's the best ___? asking for a project. tag them."
- "we redesigned one flow. [metric] moved. no new features. just UX."

### 2) AMPLIFY — Brand voice (@bricxlabs)  ← echo/repost layer
The brand account rides the founder's content instead of posting into the void.
- slightly more **polished + design-forward**, still not corporate.
- **reposts/quote-tweets** Sid's post with a one-line craft angle, or reshares an
  employee's work ("Designed at Bricx — here's the thinking 🎨"). Human-fronted wins.
- never a faceless portfolio dump.

## Gold-standard examples (real, anchor the imitation)
1. **Milestone proof** — "Second year of building my dream design agency. 2025 gave us
   23 clients, 38 projects, and a team of 9…" *(big-number hook + build-in-public + video → PEAK)*
2. **Community question** — "who's the best motion designer making SaaS launch videos
   right now? asking for a project. tag them." *(reply-driven engine, costs nothing)*
3. **Options poll** — "which hero section are you picking?" + 4 variants *(designer-native, flattering)*
4. **Employee-fronted repost** — "Designed at @bricxlabs, just for fun 🎨" *(human beat the
   corporate feed ~4×)*

## How the Ideator should use this
For each idea: borrow the **pattern** (from the taxonomy), write in the **primary
register**, ladder it to the **revenue POV**, and add a short **amplify** variant for
@bricxlabs. The pattern is borrowed; the voice and POV are Bricx's. Never copy a
source post's wording — model the *structure*, not the sentences.
